DENGUE:
Symptoms: sudden high fever, severe headache, pain behind eyes,
joint pain, muscle pain, skin rash
Red flags: bleeding gums, blood in vomit, severe stomach pain
Action: refer to PHC immediately, no aspirin or ibuprofen

TYPHOID:
Symptoms: fever that rises daily, headache, stomach pain,
constipation or loose stools, rose spots on chest
Red flags: confusion, severe stomach pain, intestinal bleeding
Action: blood test at PHC, antibiotics from doctor only

PNEUMONIA:
Symptoms: cough with yellow or green phlegm, fever, chest pain,
fast breathing, difficulty breathing
Red flags: blue lips, confusion, breathing more than 30 per minute
Action: emergency referral, especially for children and elderly

HEPATITIS:
Symptoms: tiredness, yellow skin and eyes, dark urine, nausea,
upper right stomach pain, no appetite
Red flags: severe jaundice, confusion, bleeding easily
Action: refer to PHC, rest and fluids for mild cases

IRON DEFICIENCY ANEMIA:
Symptoms: tiredness, weakness, pale skin, pale inner eyelid,
shortness of breath, dizziness, cold hands and feet
Screening: pull down lower eyelid, pale or white = anemia
Action: hemoglobin test at PHC, iron tablets, diet advice

SEVERE ACUTE MALNUTRITION:
Symptoms: visible severe wasting, bones prominent, swollen feet
and legs, MUAC below 11.5cm in children
Red flags: any child with SAM = NRC referral immediately
Action: therapeutic food RUTF, treat infections, close monitoring

HYPERTENSION:
Symptoms: often no symptoms at all, silent killer
Screening: BP above 140 over 90 on two readings = hypertension
Risk factors: excess salt, overweight, stress, family history
Action: lifestyle change, refer to PHC for medication

DIABETES:
Symptoms: frequent urination, excessive thirst, weight loss,
blurred vision, slow healing wounds, tingling in feet
Screening: random blood sugar above 200 = diabetes flag
Action: refer to PHC, lifestyle counseling
