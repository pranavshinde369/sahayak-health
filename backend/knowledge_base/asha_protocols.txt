DANGER SIGNS — IMMEDIATE PHC REFERRAL:
- Fever above 102F lasting more than 2 days
- Yellow eyes or yellow skin (jaundice)
- Pale white inner eyelid (anemia sign)
- Difficulty breathing or very fast breathing
- Severe dehydration: sunken eyes, dry mouth
- Unconsciousness or seizures
- Severe malnutrition: visible ribs, swollen feet
- Pregnant woman: heavy bleeding, swollen face, severe headache
- Child not feeding for more than 6 hours

ANEMIA SCREENING:
- Pull down lower eyelid and check inner surface
- Pink or red = normal
- Pale or white = anemia, refer to PHC for hemoglobin test
- Most common in women and children under 5
- Give iron tablets and dietary advice (green vegetables, dal)

JAUNDICE SCREENING:
- Check white part of eyes in natural light
- Yellow tint = jaundice flag, refer same day
- Dark yellow or brown urine = liver stress
- White or pale stool = bile duct problem
- Jaundice with fever = possible hepatitis, high priority

MALARIA PROTOCOL:
- Fever with chills and sweating = malaria suspicion
- Headache plus muscle pain plus high fever = malaria
- Do RDT test if available
- Positive RDT = refer to PHC same day
- Pregnant women with any fever = refer same day

MATERNAL HEALTH:
- Bleeding during pregnancy = call 108 immediately
- Swollen face and severe headache in third trimester = pre-eclampsia
- No baby movement for 12 hours = emergency
- Post-delivery fever or foul smell from wound = infection

CHILD HEALTH UNDER 5:
- Breathing more than 50 times per minute = danger
- Ribs visible when breathing = chest indrawing, danger
- Fits or convulsions = emergency
- Cannot drink or breastfeed = emergency
- Vomiting everything = danger

DEHYDRATION:
- Pinch skin on stomach, goes back slowly = dehydration
- Sunken eyes and dry mouth = moderate dehydration
- ORS: one packet in one litre of clean water
- Give small sips every 5 minutes
- Sunken eyes plus not urinating = severe, refer immediately

TUBERCULOSIS:
- Cough lasting more than 2 weeks = refer for sputum test
- Cough with blood = urgent referral today
- Night sweats plus weight loss plus cough = TB suspicion
- DOTS treatment is 6 months, never stop early

ASHA BURNOUT SIGNS:
- Constant exhaustion that sleep does not fix
- Feeling hopeless about patient outcomes
- Missing home visits without reason
- Report to supervisor immediately if these appear
